# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

This folder contains the documentation for OctoAcme's project management processes. It is intended to serve as a single source of truth for how cross-functional teams plan, execute, release, and continuously improve their work.

## Overview of OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme's project management approach is designed to make cross-functional delivery consistent, transparent, and iterative. The process begins with project initiation, where teams validate the business need, identify stakeholders, define success metrics, and decide whether work should move forward into planning. Once approved, planning translates the initiative into a prioritized backlog, estimated scope, milestones, risks, dependencies, and a release plan. Across the lifecycle, OctoAcme emphasizes customer value, clear ownership, measurable outcomes, and small, testable increments so that projects can adapt as teams learn.

Roles are clearly defined to support accountability and collaboration. Project Managers coordinate delivery, maintain timelines, manage risks, facilitate meetings, and keep communication flowing across stakeholders. Product Managers define the problem space, prioritize the roadmap and backlog, establish success criteria, and ensure the work delivers business and customer value. Developers are responsible for implementation, testing, documentation, estimation, and surfacing technical risks, while QA and stakeholders contribute validation, approvals, and feedback. Together, these roles support a lifecycle that moves from initiation to planning, execution, release, and retrospective improvement.

Execution is managed through a structured team rhythm and visible workflows. OctoAcme uses project boards with stages such as Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, and Done to track work through delivery. Teams run daily or frequent standups to discuss progress, blockers, and dependencies, hold weekly syncs to review delivery status and risks, and conduct demos or reviews at the end of sprints or milestones. Communication is treated as a core discipline: stakeholder groups are identified early, updates are shared regularly using lightweight status templates, and escalation paths move from the team level to PM, Product Lead, and sponsor when issues have broader impact.

Quality assurance is embedded throughout execution and release. OctoAcme expects clear acceptance criteria, a documented Definition of Done, unit tests for new logic, integration testing where appropriate, and end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows before release. PR practices reinforce quality by encouraging small pull requests, linking work to issues and acceptance criteria, running CI checks such as tests, linting, and security scans, and requiring approval before merge according to team policy. Releases also include pre-release checks, rollback planning, post-deploy verification, and retrospective reviews so teams can capture lessons learned and continuously improve both their delivery practices and supporting documentation.
